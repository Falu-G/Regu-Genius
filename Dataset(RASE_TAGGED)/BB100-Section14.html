Cavities in the construction of a building provide a ready route for the spread of smoke and flame. For example, hidden routes for fire spread include concealed spaces above suspended ceilings and therefore vertical fire separation must continue up to the underside of the soffit to ensure compartmentation is achieved.

To reduce the potential for fire spread, cavity barriers should be provided for both the following:

  1. to divide cavities

  2. to close the edges of cavities

Cavity barriers should be provided at all the following locations :

  1. to close the edges of cavities, including around openings (such as windows, doors and entry/exit points for services)

  2. at the junction between an external cavity wall and every compartment floor and compartment wall (except where the cavity wall complies with Figure 12 )

  3. at the junction between an internal cavity wall and every compartment floor, compartment wall, or other wall or door assembly that forms a fire- resisting barrier (except where the cavity wall complies with Figure 12 )

Compartment walls should be carried up full storey height to a compartment floor or to the roof as appropriate (i.e. through a ceiling void or roof cavity) to maintain the standard of fire separation. A line of compartmentation cannot be completed by fitting cavity barriers above them .

For a protected escape route , a cavity that exists above or below any fire-resisting construction (because the construction is not carried to full storey height or, in the case of a top storey, to the underside of the roof covering) should either be:

  1. fitted with cavity barriers on the line of the enclosure(s) to the protected escape route ; or

  2. for cavities above the fire-resisting construction , enclosed on the lower side by a fire-resisting ceiling that extends throughout the building, compartment or separated part

In Figure 13a, cavity barriers need not be provided where the sheeting is rated Class A2-s3, d2 or better and both surfaces of the insulating layer rated Class C- s3, d2 or better .

Location of cavity Class Maximum dimensions in any direction (m)
Between a roof and a ceiling Any class 20
Any other cavity Class C-s3, d2 or better 20
Any other cavity Worse than Class C-s3, d2 10

The provisions in Table 10 do not apply to any cavity that is:

  1. in a wall which should be fire-resisting only because it is loadbearing

  2. in a masonry or concrete external cavity wall shown in Figure 12

  3. a cavity that meets the conditions of Figure 13a

  4. formed behind the external skin of an external cladding system with a masonry or concrete inner leaf at least 75mm thick , or by over-cladding an existing masonry (or concrete ) external wall, or an existing concrete roof , provided that the cavity does not contain materials achieving Class B-s3,d2 or worse

  5. below a floor next to the ground or oversite concrete , if the cavity is less than 1000mm in height or if the cavity is not normally accessible by persons , unless there are openings in the floor such that it is possible for combustibles to accumulate in the cavity (in which case cavity barriers should be provided and access should be provided to the cavity for cleaning )

Where any single room with a ceiling cavity or underfloor service void exceeds the dimensions given in Table 10 , cavity barriers need only be provided on the line of the enclosing walls / partitions of that room, subject to:

  1. the cavity barriers being no more than 40m apart

  2. the surface of the material/product exposed in the cavity being Class C- s3, d2 or better

Where the concealed space is an undivided area that exceeds 40m (this may be in both directions on plan) there is no limit to the size of the cavity if:

  1. the room and the cavity together are compartmented from the rest of the building

  2. an automatic fire detection and alarm system meeting the relevant recommendations of BS 5839-1 is fitted in the building. Detectors are only required in the cavity to satisfy BS 5839-1

  3. the cavity is used as a plenum and the recommendations about recirculating air distribution systems in BS 9999 Section 32.5.3, "air handling voids" are followed

  4. the surface of the material/product used in the construction of the cavity which is exposed in the cavity is Class B-s3, d2 or better and the supports and fixings in the cavity are Class A1

  5. the flame spread rating of any pipe insulation system is Class C-s3, d2 or better

  6. any electrical wiring in the void is laid in metal trays , or in metalconduit

  7. any other materials in the cavity are rated Class A2-s3, d2 or better

Every cavity barrier should be constructed to provide at least 30 minutes fire resistance - 30 minutes integrity (E30) and 15 minutes insulation (I15) . Cavity barriers in a stud wall or partition, or provided around openings may be formed of:

  1. steel at least 0.5mm thick

  2. timber at least 38mm thick

  3. polythene-sleeved mineral wool , or mineral wool slab, in either case under compression when installed in the cavity, or

  4. calcium silicate , cement-based or gypsum-based boards at least 12mm thick

Cavity barriers around openings may be formed by window or door frames if the frame is constructed of steel or timber of at least the minimum thickness in a and b above .

A cavity barrier should, wherever possible, be tightly fitted to a rigid construction and mechanically fixed in position . Where this is not possible (for example, in the case of a junction with slates, tiles, corrugated sheeting or similar materials) the junction should be fire-stopped .

Cavity barriers should also be fixed so that their performance is unlikely to be made ineffective by:

  1. movement of the building due to subsidence, shrinkage or temperature change and movement of the external envelope due to wind

  2. collapse in a fire of any services penetrating them

  3. failure in a fire of their fixings (but see note below)

  4. failure in a fire of any material or construction that they abut

Note: Where cavity barriers are provided in roof spaces, the roof members to which they are fitted are not expected to have any fire resistance for supporting the cavity barrier(s).

Any openings in a cavity barrier should be limited to those for:

  1. doors which have at least 30 minutes fire resistance

  2. the passage of pipes which meet the provisions in Section 15

  3. the passage of cables or conduits containing one or more cables

  4. openings fitted with a suitably mounted automatic fire damper

  5. ducts , which (unless they are fire-resisting ) are fitted with a suitably mounted automatic fire damper where they pass through the cavity barrier
